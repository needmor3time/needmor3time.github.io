Portfolio

Below is a sampling of the applications I've created as part of my Full Stack Developer Boot Camp at the University of Denver. The list is in chronological order starting with the latest at the top, so bear in mind that the least simpliest apps are at the bottom :-) Note that none of these apps have been paid for by any government body or company.



The Swift Event application was our first group project. The assignment called for us to use at least two api's and a new technology. Our api's were googlemaps and eventful. We decided to go with an application that you can do a search based on location of things that are 'going on'. They range from gun shows to quilting, movies, and concerts. The results of the search are displayed 3 at a time with a maximum of three pages.



The train tracker application was one of our homework assignments that would display existing train details and has a section for new trains to be added.



This homework assignment proved to be rather challeging for me and it's been reworked somewhat from what was originally turned in. The requirements were to make a trivia application that had a time out feature and would keep track of your correct and incorrect answers. One of our TA's said "make it hard", and so it is. Most people only get about 2 or 3 correct answers out of 12 (and I think most of those are lucky guesses :-) ).



This assignment was one of our earlier assignments and I probably spent the most time on it. I had to just about re-write it based on how I thought the code "should work". It's a simple hang man game with a Greek Gods theme, it gives the answer at the end regardless of winning or losing.
